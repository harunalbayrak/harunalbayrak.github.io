I've been thinking about the tools I use every day. Some of them I barely notice. Others fight me at every turn. The difference isn't capability — it's design.

## The Tools That Disappear

The best tools are the ones you forget you're using. They become an extension of thought. Your editor, your terminal, your version control — when they work well, they vanish into the background of your workflow.

## The Tools That Fight Back

Then there are the tools that demand your attention. Complex configuration. Opinions about how you should work. Plugins upon plugins. Each layer adds friction.

The measure of a good tool isn't how many features it has. It's how quickly you can stop thinking about it and get back to the actual work.
